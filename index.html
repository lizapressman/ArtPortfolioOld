What I Do

My work primarily addresses my personal experiences and journeys. I have always struggled to convey my thoughts and feelings through words so, from a young age, I used art as an alternative way to express myself. 
I use a wide variety of mediums and as I am so early in my art career, a lot of my work is exploratory. I enjoys playing with found objects both because I am a broke college student and because it opens my work up to a lot of unexpected meaning through the history, use, and purpose of the objects. I play with the importance of material and how the medium and marks relate to the different themes and stories. This relation of material to meaning is the driving force behind much of my work. A lot of my work has been completed for student exhibitions and thus, has been tied to specific assignment themes such as mapping, control, autobiography, etc.
